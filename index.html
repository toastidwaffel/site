About The Game

Catastrophic Inversion is a multiplayer VR arena shooter playable at full-scale in any gym or other large space. You really walk around the space without joysticks, teleporting, or other unnatural-feeling locomotion.


It comes with 10 arenas (and counting) with moving parts like elevators to take you to second-story platforms or gondolas to carry you across large chasms, all while allowing multiple players to move around within the same playspace. (We've tested up to 10 players but there's no hard limit.)


Calibration is handled entirely within the Quest headset and requires no external sensors or equipment (other than adequate Wi-Fi coverage for a local network connection between devices). Setup involves a one-time walk around the perimeter of the space by the host and definition of 2 calibration points to be touched by all players to align the headsets.

Recommended Setup

  • A few Meta Quest 2, 3, or 3S headsets with the Meta Quest edition of the game.
  • A 14m x 24m playspace (79' x 46') or larger. (There are also some simpler 10m x 10m maps if you don't have that much space.)
  • Consistent Wi-Fi coverage throughout your space. (Internet access recommended, but not required.)
  • A mid-range gaming laptop with the Steam edition of the game as a dedicated server and spectator feed. (A headset could host if needed, but it's less reliable.')
